  3. Definitions of Types of Information

    The Foundation collects different types of information from users, including personally identifiable information (PII) such as your name, mailing and email addresses, financial data, and demographic data including age and zip code. PII is information that can specifically identify you.

    Traffic Data is also collected automatically, such as IP address, browser type, domain server, and computer type, which is anonymous and used for analytics and improving user experience.

    Cookies and similar technologies may be used to collect Traffic Data and remember preferences.

  4. Collection and Use of Personally Identifiable Information

    * Registration *

    Some parts of the Site may require users to provide personal information voluntarily. Users are never obligated to provide such information, but some features may be inaccessible without it.

    * Sharing Personally Identifiable Information *

    The Foundation may share personal information with trusted service providers to help operate the Site. Personal information will not be sold without explicit consent.

    * Legal Disclosures *

    Personal information may be disclosed to comply with laws, enforce the Terms of Use, or protect the rights of the Foundation.

    * Traffic Data *

    Traffic Data is collected for analysis of usage patterns. It may be shared with partners but does not personally identify users.

    * Cookies *

    Cookies are small files stored on your device that help enhance site functionality. Disabling cookies may impact features.

    * Children’s Privacy *

    The Site is not intended for children under 13. Personal information from minors is not knowingly collected without parental consent, in compliance with COPPA.

    * IP Addresses *

    IP addresses may be collected for system administration and security purposes and are not linked to PII unless required to enforce Site policies.

    * Third-Party Relationships *

    Some links or services may be provided by third parties. The Foundation is not responsible for their privacy policies. Contact the Foundation if you have concerns.

    * Security *

    Industry-standard security measures are used to protect data, but absolute security cannot be guaranteed. Unauthorized account use should be reported immediately.

  6. Do Not Sell My Personal Information

    * Global Privacy Rights *

    The Foundation respects privacy rights worldwide, including GDPR, PIPEDA, and other applicable laws.

    * How to Exercise Your Rights *

    To exercise privacy rights (access, correction, deletion, objection, or portability), contact the Foundation via Contact Me.

    * California Privacy Rights *

    Under CCPA/CPRA, California residents may opt out of sale or sharing of personal information. The Foundation does not sell personal information for monetary consideration.

    * How to Opt Out *

    California residents may contact Contact Me to opt out of sale or sharing of personal data.

    * Accessibility Support *

    For assistance with privacy rights requests, contact Contact Me.
