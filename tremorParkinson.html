Tremors vs. Parkinson’s Disease

Essential tremor and Parkinson’s disease are different neurological conditions. Tremors in essential tremor are typically action-based, whereas Parkinson’s tremors often occur at rest. Understanding the differences helps patients, families, and caregivers manage symptoms effectively.

Essential Tremor
Essential Tremor

Action tremors that worsen with movement. Can affect the hands, head, or voice. Often hereditary and progresses gradually.

Parkinson’s Tremor
Parkinson’s Tremor

Resting tremors, usually start on one side, may progress with rigidity and slowed movements.

Key Differences
Key Differences

Essential tremor is usually hereditary and action-based; Parkinson’s tremor often occurs at rest with additional motor symptoms.
